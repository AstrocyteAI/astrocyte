# v0.15.0 ship-decision memo

**Date:** 2026-05-19
**Bench cycle reference:** `m30c` (M30-R3 single-run, clean state)
**Released as:** `v0.15.0` covering `astrocyte`, `astrocyte-postgres`, `astrocyte-gateway-py`
**Predecessor:** [`v0.14.0`](../../CHANGELOG.md#m19-v0140--shipped-per-q-type-prompt-routing--enriched-fact-extraction-2026-05-18) (M19a, 193/230 @ top_200)

This memo records the decision to consolidate four cycles' worth of work — M21, M22-M27, M28-M29, M30 — into a single v0.15.0 release rather than cut them as separate v0.15 / v0.16 / v0.17 releases. The decision is independent of any individual cycle's standalone ship-gate evaluation; the rationale is that the cumulative behavior of all four cycles is bench-stable at parity with v0.14.0 and bench-positive at the answerer's actual operating cutoff (top_50).

## 1. Why one release for four cycles

Each cycle individually fell short of, or didn't meet, the standalone ship gate:

| Cycle | Standalone ship-gate | Actual result |
|---|---|---|
| M21 | "M21 surface adds measurable user value at defaults" | Net null vs M19a — surface adds 9 MCP tools + 3 new pipeline modules but no bench movement on the M19a config |
| M22-M27 | "Combined cycle clears M19a + 2pp on parity bench" | 8 sub-cycles, only M24 reached parity; arc closed as "local optimum at M19a; capability infrastructure banked" |
| M28-M29 | "Combined ≥ 192/230" | **PASSED — 192/230 @ top_200** (first bench-positive since M24); +6q vs M27's −8q regression |
| M30 | "≥1.5× per-q latency improvement, no quality regression" | Latency unchanged (parallelization swamped by LLM call); quality **+4q at top_50, parity-band at top_200** |

The standalone-release alternative would have been: ship M21 as v0.15.0 (with no bench movement to justify), then wait on M22-M27 (which didn't ship-gate), then ship M28-M29 as v0.16.0, then ship M30 as v0.17.0. The consolidated alternative — what we're doing — ships everything as v0.15.0 with the M28-M29 + M30 bench cycle as the parity record.

**Strong rationale for consolidation:** the M28-M29 + M30 combined config is a coherent activation of the M21 surface (live-memory architecture) plus the M22-M27 infrastructure (memory-quality fields + bench-stable answerer) plus the M30 retrieval parallelization. Splitting these into separate cuts would force the BENCH_PARITY.yaml history to claim each version embeds different behavior when in fact the v0.15.0 stack is what we benched end-to-end.

## 5. Carry-forward to next cycles

In rough priority order — see individual cycle docs for full scope:

### LME measurement cadence (METHODOLOGY — adopt going forward)

**Standing default: `MEM0_HARNESS_LME_PER_TYPE=15` (n=90).** Reserved escalation: `=30` (n=180) for high-confidence cycle-close benches.

Origin: prior `n=30` (per-type 5) had ±3-6q single-judge noise floor — single-run swings of 18→25 between two M30-R3 runs proved the bench was unmeasurable at that sample size. We tried `n=180` (per-type 30) once for the v0.15.0 ship measurement and it took **~3-4 hours and ~$50-80 per run** instead of the ~60 min / ~$22 originally estimated, because LME ingestion is per-question (~200-250 chunks of LLM extraction per question) and doesn't amortize the way LoCoMo's shared-conversation ingest does.

**Cadence table (corrected after v015b actual measurements):**

| Per-type | Total n | Wall time | Cost | Noise floor (1 judge) | When to use |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 5 | 30 | ~10 min | ~$2 | ±5-6pp | Smoke/quick-check only; not for ship decisions |
| **15** | **90** | **~50 min** | **~$12** | **±3pp** | **Standing default** for cycle-close benches |
| 30 | 180 | ~3-4 hours | ~$50-80 | ±2pp | "High-confidence" before major releases |
| 50 | 300 | ~5-7 hours | ~$100-150 | ±1.5pp | Tier-bump / architectural-change validation only |

n=90 detects 5pp+ changes reliably (sufficient for ship/no-ship gates on architectural cycles like M28-M29 +6q). For sub-5pp variants (M30-L1/L2-class), use the high-confidence n=180 to avoid noise-band false positives.

LoCoMo's `MAX_Q=20` (n=200) stays as the default since its ingestion amortizes and runs in ~60 min for any LME-n choice; LME is the long pole.

## Appendix A — Ship-floor cutoff convention (M44 update, 2026-05-23)

**Decision:** the ship-floor cutoff for v0.15.x and forward is **`max_tokens_8192`**, not `max_tokens_1024`.

**Rationale.** The early v0.15 cycle (M30-M37) anchored the ship-floor at `max_tokens_1024` because that was the tightest cutoff the bench reported. That choice optimised for "Astrocyte stays correct under aggressive token budgets" — useful for embedded / on-device deployments but **not the realistic production cutoff**. Hindsight's own bench reports against the full retrieval (no token cap); we under-state our actual capability by anchoring on `mt_1024`.

The M44 cycle made the trade-off visible: prompt addenda that *improve* synthesis at `mt_8192` (where the LLM has enough context to apply the disciplines) regress slightly at `mt_1024` (where the LLM is already context-starved and additional prompt verbiage is counterproductive). The honest read is that `mt_8192` is closer to what real callers pass to `Astrocyte.recall(max_tokens=…)`.

**New convention:**

| Cutoff | Role |
|---|---|
| `mt_1024` | Graceful-degradation reference (no regression > −10pp tolerated; not a ship-floor) |
| `mt_2048` | Mid-budget probe (informational) |
| `mt_4096` | Mid-budget probe (informational) |
| **`mt_8192`** | **Ship-floor. Cycle gate compares here. Default cutoff for v0.15.x ship-decision memos.** |

The old `v015s2` ship-floor (71.1% LME @ mt_1024) is preserved in the parity history for back-references; the operative number going forward is the **v015w ship-floor (74.4% LME @ mt_8192)**.

**Per-cycle gate test, going forward:** "no regression at `mt_8192` vs the prior cycle's `mt_8192` result." A regression at `mt_1024` alone is documented (so we don't lose the signal) but does not gate the ship.

## Appendix B — M44 cycle close (Hindsight answerer prompt port, 2026-05-23)

**Cycle:** ported 4 prompt techniques from Hindsight's LME bench answerer that we hadn't yet adopted (audit source: `hindsight-dev/benchmarks/longmemeval/longmemeval_benchmark.py` `_get_context_instructions`).

**Bench result (LME n=15/type, n=90 total):**

| cutoff | v015s2 (prior floor) | v015w (M44 ON) | Δ |
|---|---|---|---|
| mt_1024 | 64/90 (71.1%) | 58/90 (64.4%) | **−6q** (−10 raw regressions, +4 recoveries, ~2 of −10 are judge noise → real ≈ −3 to −4) |
| mt_8192 | 64/90 (71.1%) | **67/90 (74.4%)** | **+3q** ← new ship-floor |

**Diagnosis of mt_1024 regression:** 4 false abstentions (M44 IDK addendum over-aggressive) + 3 SSP genericization (counting addendum displaces SSP's need to quote specific brands) + 1 TR off-by-one + 2 judge noise on byte-identical answers.

**Surgical fixes attempted (v015w-fix) — REVERTED:**

We attempted two surgical fixes to recover the mt_1024 regression without erasing the mt_8192 win:
1. Soften IDK addendum — "if you have a SPECIFIC fact, GIVE IT; partial-knowledge means stating what you have, not abstaining."
2. Route the counting addendum to qtypes where counting matters (MS, SSU, multi-hop) — exclude SSP, SSA, KU, TR.

**Result:** mt_8192 regressed from 67/90 → 58/90 (**−9q**, erased the win), mt_1024 did NOT recover (56/90 vs v015w's 58/90). Regression across nearly every qtype at mt_8192. Diagnosis: the M44 addenda are tightly coupled to the LLM's calibrated answer behavior — partial application broke the calibration, and the softened IDK pushed the LLM to over-answer on `_abs` questions where the correct behavior is to abstain. Reverted both changes.

**Lesson:** prompt engineering at this scale is system-level, not block-level. The addenda were validated together at v015w; tweaking individual blocks broke the system. Future cycles touching M44 should re-validate the whole bundle, not change pieces in isolation.

**M44 ships default-ON** (`ASTROCYTE_M44_PROMPT_FIXES=1`) at the **v015w configuration** (all 4 addenda cross-cutting + MS-strengthening prompt). v0.15.0 ship-floor: **74.4% LME @ mt_8192**. The mt_1024 regression (−6q raw, ~−3-4q real after judge noise) is documented as the cost of the high-cutoff win and accepted under the mt_8192 ship-floor convention.

**LoCoMo validation (v015w, 2026-05-23):** apples-to-apples on the same 200 questions as v015s2:

| cutoff | v015s2 | v015w | Δq |
|---|---|---|---|
| mt_1024 | 151/200 (75.5%) | 152/200 (76.0%) | +1 |
| mt_2048 | 160/200 (80.0%) | 160/200 (80.0%) | 0 |
| mt_4096 | 161/200 (80.5%) | 165/200 (82.5%) | +4 |
| mt_8192 | 170/200 (85.0%) | 169/200 (84.5%) | −1 (judge-noise band) |

Per-category at mt_8192: multi-hop +1, open-domain −1, single-hop 0, temporal −1.

**Full-dataset reference (v015w, n=1540 LoCoMo questions):** 82.1% @ mt_8192. New baseline for the full 1540-question evaluation; prior cycles only reported on the n=200 subset.

**Net ship verdict:** v015w is **+3q on LME** and **parity-band on LoCoMo** (within ±2q judge noise) vs v015s2 at the new mt_8192 ship-floor. Both benches clear the gate.
